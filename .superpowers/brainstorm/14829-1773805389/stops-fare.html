How should stops & fare work?

Customers add stops mid-route — the meter adjusts. Which model fits how you run the business?

A

Quoted upfront, adjusted at end

Customer gets an estimated fare before booking. If they add stops or the route changes, the driver records them during the ride. At completion, the final fare is recalculated based on actual km driven + stop surcharges. Customer is shown the final total before paying.

  • Most like Uber / Lyft
  • Customer knows approx cost upfront
  • Final charge may differ from estimate
  • Driver taps "Add Stop" during ride
B

Multi-stop planned at booking

Customer adds all stops before confirming the booking. Up to 3 intermediate stops. Fare is calculated across the full route (pickup → stop 1 → stop 2 → dropoff) and shown as one total. No surprises at the end.

  • Transparent — full price shown before booking
  • Good for planned trips (medical, airport, errands)
  • Driver gets the full route in advance
  • Each stop adds $3–5 stop fee + km between stops
C

Both — plan stops or add them live

Customer can add stops at booking time and the driver can add unplanned stops during the ride. Upfront estimate covers planned stops. Any additional stops are added to the final total. Most flexible.

  • Maximum flexibility for customer & driver
  • Estimate shown for planned route
  • Final receipt shows itemised breakdown
  • Most complex to build — but the most professional
